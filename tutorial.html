Materials

Acquire the following:

1. Diodes

1N4148 diodes (one for each switch)

2. Switches

Many switch types available, choose based off of feel/purpose/sound

Ultimate Guide to Mechanical Switches

3. Keycaps

Choose based off of aesthetics, make sure the set you're buying contains enough keys

4. Stabilizers

Two types of stabilizers currently exist; Costar and Cherry MX. All keyboards sold in my shop (except Clueboard 66%) use costar stabilizers.

5. Micro-Controller

Two types of Micro-Controllers are common; Teensy 2.0 and Teensy++ 2.0. The Teensy 2.0 will work for all keyboard types except full size and tkl. The larger keyboards will require a Teensy++ 2.0.

6. Nuts and Bolts

All keyboards (except clueboard) require 3M x 8mm hex screws. Amount is dependent on keyboard, but will at most be 24.

7. Optional: Soldering Iron

Quality of soldering iron quality does not matter, they all work well in the right hands. Just make sure its the "pencil" style of iron, and not the "gun" style. The pencil style is for soldering electronics, the gun style is for scorching designs in wood.

8. Optional: Solder

60-40 rosin core solder works best

9. Optional: Solder Sucker

Again, quality does not matter too much

Soldering

Many find this step difficult, but I belive in you

Quick Guide

1. Heat up iron to 350-450 degrees

2. Place tip or iron so it touches both joints that need to be soldered

3. Press solder wire to point where iron and joints meet, solder should melt and be "magnatized" toward joints

4. Remove iron and solder wire, let joint harden
